Career Copilot

Problem: Students and new grads often have to rewrite their resumes for different jobs, but a lot of resume advice is either too generic or pushes people toward exaggerating their experience.

Contribution: I built Career Copilot as a full-stack web app. Users can upload a resume, save a job posting, compare the two, and generate resume bullets or cover letters that stay based on their actual experience.

Tools: Next.js, React, TypeScript, Prisma, Supabase Postgres, Clerk, Google Gemini, AI SDK, Tailwind CSS, and Vercel.

Constraints: The AI output had to stay useful without making up qualifications. I wanted the app to improve how users frame their experience, not invent experience they do not have.

Status: The app is deployed and has a working dashboard, resume upload flow, job management, match analysis, resume rewrite support, and cover letter generation.

What I learned: This project taught me how much structure matters in AI products. I had to think about database models, authentication, saved user data, prompts, and the steps a user would actually go through while applying for a job.
